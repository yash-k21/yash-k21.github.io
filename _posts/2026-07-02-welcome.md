I'm starting this blog as a place to write about things I'm actively learning. The plan is simple: whenever a concept in **econometrics**, **statistics**, or **machine learning** finally clicks — or stubbornly doesn't — I'll write it up here.

Writing forces a kind of honesty that reading doesn't. It's easy to nod along to a derivation; it's much harder to explain it cleanly to someone else (or to your future self). So these posts are as much for me as for anyone who stumbles across them.

### What to expect

- **Econometrics** — identification, causal inference, panel data, and the assumptions we quietly rely on.
- **Statistics** — estimation, inference, and the intuition behind the methods I use.
- **Machine learning** — models, training dynamics, and where the theory meets practice.

Expect a mix of short notes, worked examples, and the occasional longer deep-dive. Math is rendered with MathJax, so I can write things like

$$
\hat{\beta} = (X^\top X)^{-1} X^\top y
$$

when it helps.

More to come soon.
